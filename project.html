How the System Finds Information

The system uses different search methods depending on your question. It automatically picks the best method, but here's what happens behind the scenes:

Meaning-Based Search

Best for: Questions where you want documents that mean the same thing, even if they use different words

Example: You search for "car accident" and the system also finds documents about "vehicle collision" or "auto crash" because it understands they mean similar things.

How it works: The system converts your question and all documents into mathematical patterns that capture their meaning, then finds patterns that are most similar to your question.

Relationship-Based Search

Best for: Questions about how things are connected or related

Example: You ask "Who works with John Smith?" and the system finds connections between people, projects, and departments mentioned in your documents.

How it works: The system builds a map of how people, places, and concepts in your documents are connected, like a social network graph, then traces paths through these connections.

Combined Search (Recommended)

Best for: Most questions - provides the most reliable results

Example: You ask "What are our safety policies for warehouse equipment?" and the system uses both meaning-based and relationship-based searches, then ranks all results to show you the best matches.

How it works: Combines multiple search methods and intelligently ranks the results, ensuring you get the most relevant information regardless of how your documents are written.

Answer-First Search

Best for: Complex questions where you need expert-level information

Example: You ask "What causes engine overheating?" The system first imagines what a good answer would look like, then searches for documents that match that imagined answer.

How it works: Instead of searching for your question directly, the system generates a hypothetical answer first, then finds documents that are similar to that answer.

Multi-Part Search

Best for: Complex questions with multiple parts

Example: You ask "What are our hiring procedures and salary ranges for engineers?" The system breaks this into two questions: one about hiring and one about salaries, searches for each separately, then combines the results.

How it works: Splits complex questions into simpler sub-questions, searches for each independently, then assembles all findings into one comprehensive answer.
